Cosine (cos θ)

Definition

The cosine function is a fundamental trigonometric function. In a right triangle, cosine of an angle θ is defined as the ratio of the length of the adjacent side to the length of the hypotenuse.

Formula: cos θ = adjacent / hypotenuse

Understanding Cosine

Cosine represents the horizontal component of a point on a unit circle. In a right triangle:

  • The adjacent side is the side next to angle θ (but not the hypotenuse)
  • The hypotenuse is the longest side, opposite the right angle
  • cos θ gives you the ratio between the adjacent side and hypotenuse

Key Properties

  • Range: cos θ ranges from -1 to 1
  • Maximum: cos θ = 1 when θ = 0° (or 0 radians) + 360°k
  • Minimum: cos θ = -1 when θ = 180° (or π radians) + 360°k
  • Zero points: cos θ = 0 when θ = 90°, 270°, etc. (or π/2, 3π/2 radians)
  • Period: The cosine function repeats every 360° (or 2π radians)

Applications

  • Vector Projection: Projects any vector onto the horizontal axis
  • Oscillations: Models phase shifts in wave motion and AC circuits
  • Geometry: Used in coordinate systems and rotations
  • Physics: Component analysis in forces and motion

Graph Characteristics

The cosine graph is a smooth, continuous wave that:

  • Starts at maximum (1) at 0°
  • Drops to zero at 90°
  • Reaches minimum (-1) at 180°
  • Returns to zero at 270°
  • Completes the cycle at 360°

Note: The cosine graph is the sine graph shifted 90° to the left.

Important Identities

  • cos²θ + sin²θ = 1 (Pythagorean identity)
  • cos(-θ) = cos θ (even function)
  • cos(90° - θ) = sin θ
  • cos(θ + 360°) = cos θ (periodic)

Relationship with Sine

Cosine and sine are closely related:

  • They are 90° out of phase: cos θ = sin(θ + 90°)
  • Together they satisfy: cos²θ + sin²θ = 1
  • In a unit circle, if sin θ gives the y-coordinate, cos θ gives the x-coordinate

Example

In a right triangle with angle θ = 60°:

  • If the adjacent side = 3 units and hypotenuse = 6 units
  • Then cos(60°) = 3/6 = 0.5
  • This is a standard value: cos(60°) = 1/2
