Linear Equations

Definition

A linear equation is an equation where the highest power of the variable is 1. The graph of a linear equation is always a straight line.

Standard Form: y = mx + b
Where: m = slope, b = y-intercept

Key Components

  • Slope (m): Measures the steepness of the line. It tells you how much y changes for every 1 unit change in x.
  • Y-intercept (b): The point where the line crosses the y-axis (when x = 0).
  • Variables: x (independent) and y (dependent)

Understanding Slope

The slope m represents the rate of change:

  • Positive slope: Line goes up from left to right
  • Negative slope: Line goes down from left to right
  • Zero slope: Horizontal line (y = constant)
  • Undefined slope: Vertical line (x = constant)
m = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁) = rise / run

Forms of Linear Equations

Slope-Intercept Form

y = mx + b

Most common form, easy to graph.

Point-Slope Form

y - y₁ = m(x - x₁)

Useful when you know a point and the slope.

Standard Form

Ax + By = C

Where A, B, and C are integers, and A is positive.

Solving Linear Equations

Single Variable

Example: Solve 3x + 5 = 14

  • Subtract 5: 3x = 9
  • Divide by 3: x = 3

Systems of Linear Equations

When you have multiple equations with multiple variables:

Method 1: Substitution

Solve one equation for a variable, then substitute into the other.

Method 2: Elimination

Add or subtract equations to eliminate a variable.

Method 3: Matrix Method

Use matrices and row operations (for advanced problems).

Graphing Linear Equations

  1. Identify the y-intercept (b) and plot it
  2. Use the slope (m) to find another point: move right by the denominator, up/down by the numerator
  3. Draw a line through the points

Applications

  • Economics: Cost functions, revenue models
  • Physics: Motion with constant velocity (distance = speed × time)
  • Engineering: Linear relationships in circuits and structures
  • Business: Break-even analysis, profit calculations

Example Problem

Problem: A taxi charges $3 base fare plus $2 per mile. Write an equation and find the cost for 5 miles.

Solution:

  • Equation: y = 2x + 3 (where x = miles, y = cost)
  • For 5 miles: y = 2(5) + 3 = $13

Key Tips

  • Always check your solution by substituting back into the original equation
  • For systems, the solution is the point where the lines intersect
  • Parallel lines have the same slope but different y-intercepts (no solution)
  • Coincident lines are the same line (infinite solutions)
