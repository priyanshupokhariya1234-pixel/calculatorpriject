Estimation Tricks

Introduction

Estimation is the skill of quickly approximating calculations without exact computation. It's invaluable for checking calculator work, making quick decisions, and understanding if answers are reasonable.

Rounding Strategies

Round to Nearest Convenient Number

  • Round to nearest 10, 100, or 1000
  • Example: 47 × 23 ≈ 50 × 20 = 1000
  • Actual: 47 × 23 = 1081 (very close!)

Compatible Numbers

Use numbers that are easy to work with mentally:

  • 25 × 4 = 100
  • 50 × 2 = 100
  • 125 × 8 = 1000

Example: 24 × 48 ≈ 25 × 50 = 1250

Multiplication Tricks

Multiplying by Powers of 10

  • × 10: Move decimal right 1 place (or add a zero)
  • × 100: Move decimal right 2 places
  • × 1000: Move decimal right 3 places

Multiplying by 5

Multiply by 10, then divide by 2

  • 47 × 5 = 47 × 10 ÷ 2 = 470 ÷ 2 = 235

Multiplying by 11

For two-digit numbers: add the digits, put sum in the middle

  • 23 × 11: 2 (2+3) 3 = 253
  • If sum > 9, carry the 1

Multiplying by 9

Multiply by 10, then subtract the number

  • 47 × 9 = 47 × 10 - 47 = 470 - 47 = 423

Division Tricks

Dividing by 5

Multiply by 2, then divide by 10

  • 47 ÷ 5 = 47 × 2 ÷ 10 = 94 ÷ 10 = 9.4

Dividing by 25

Multiply by 4, then divide by 100

  • 200 ÷ 25 = 200 × 4 ÷ 100 = 800 ÷ 100 = 8

Percentage Estimation

10% Rule

10% of any number = divide by 10 (move decimal left 1 place)

  • 10% of 350 = 35
  • Then: 20% = 2 × 10%, 30% = 3 × 10%, etc.

50% Rule

50% = divide by 2

  • 50% of 240 = 120

25% Rule

25% = divide by 4 (or half of 50%)

  • 25% of 200 = 50

Square Number Estimation

Near Perfect Squares

If you know n², estimate (n+1)²:

  • 30² = 900
  • 31² ≈ 900 + 30 + 31 = 961 (actual: 961)

Ending in 5

For numbers ending in 5:

  • 25²: 2 × 3 = 6, then add 25 → 625
  • 35²: 3 × 4 = 12, then add 25 → 1225

Fraction Estimation

Comparing Fractions

Cross-multiply to compare:

  • Is 3/7 or 4/9 larger?
  • 3 × 9 = 27, 4 × 7 = 28
  • Since 28 > 27, 4/9 is larger

Order of Magnitude

Quickly estimate the scale:

  • Is the answer in hundreds, thousands, or millions?
  • Example: 47 × 23 is definitely in the thousands (not hundreds or tens of thousands)

Error Checking

Use estimation to catch calculator mistakes:

  • If calculator says 47 × 23 = 47, you know it's wrong (should be ~1000)
  • If calculator says 47 × 23 = 10,810, you know it's wrong (should be ~1000)

Real-World Applications

  • Shopping: Estimate total cost before checkout
  • Cooking: Adjust recipe quantities
  • Travel: Estimate time and distance
  • Budgeting: Quick financial calculations
  • Science: Check if experimental results are reasonable

Tips for Better Estimation

  • Round numbers to make calculations easier
  • Use compatible numbers (numbers that work well together)
  • Estimate before calculating exactly
  • Check if your estimate makes sense
  • Practice mental math regularly
  • Learn common number patterns (squares, cubes, etc.)

Example: Shopping Estimate

Items: $12.99, $8.50, $24.75, $5.25

  • Round: $13 + $9 + $25 + $5 = $52
  • Actual: $51.49 (very close!)

Example: Tip Calculation

Bill: $47.80, want to tip 20%

  • 10% = $4.78 ≈ $5
  • 20% = 2 × $5 = $10
  • Actual: $9.56 (close enough for estimation!)
