Sine (sin θ)

Definition

The sine function is one of the fundamental trigonometric functions. In a right triangle, sine of an angle θ is defined as the ratio of the length of the side opposite to the angle to the length of the hypotenuse.

Formula: sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse

Understanding Sine

Sine represents the vertical component of a point on a unit circle. When you have a right triangle:

  • The opposite side is the side directly across from angle θ
  • The hypotenuse is the longest side, opposite the right angle
  • sin θ gives you the ratio between these two sides

Key Properties

  • Range: sin θ ranges from -1 to 1
  • Maximum: sin θ = 1 when θ = 90° (or π/2 radians) + 360°k
  • Minimum: sin θ = -1 when θ = 270° (or 3π/2 radians) + 360°k
  • Zero points: sin θ = 0 when θ = 0°, 180°, 360°, etc. (or 0, π, 2π radians)
  • Period: The sine function repeats every 360° (or 2π radians)

Applications

  • Wave Motion: Sine waves model oscillations, sound waves, and light waves
  • Physics: Used in simple harmonic motion, alternating current (AC) circuits
  • Engineering: Signal processing, vibration analysis
  • Navigation: Calculating heights and distances

Graph Characteristics

The sine graph is a smooth, continuous wave that:

  • Starts at (0, 0)
  • Rises to maximum at 90°
  • Returns to zero at 180°
  • Drops to minimum at 270°
  • Completes the cycle at 360°

Important Identities

  • sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 (Pythagorean identity)
  • sin(-θ) = -sin θ (odd function)
  • sin(90° - θ) = cos θ
  • sin(θ + 360°) = sin θ (periodic)

Example

In a right triangle with angle θ = 30°:

  • If the opposite side = 5 units and hypotenuse = 10 units
  • Then sin(30°) = 5/10 = 0.5
  • This is a standard value: sin(30°) = 1/2
