Angle Identities

Introduction

Angle identities are formulas that allow you to break down complex trigonometric expressions into simpler ones. They are essential tools for solving trigonometric equations and simplifying calculations.

Addition and Subtraction Formulas

These formulas help you find trigonometric values for sums and differences of angles:

Sine Addition: sin(α + β) = sin α cos β + cos α sin β
Sine Subtraction: sin(α - β) = sin α cos β - cos α sin β
Cosine Addition: cos(α + β) = cos α cos β - sin α sin β
Cosine Subtraction: cos(α - β) = cos α cos β + sin α sin β
Tangent Addition: tan(α + β) = (tan α + tan β) / (1 - tan α tan β)
Tangent Subtraction: tan(α - β) = (tan α - tan β) / (1 + tan α tan β)

Double Angle Formulas

These express trigonometric functions of 2θ in terms of θ:

sin(2θ) = 2 sin θ cos θ
cos(2θ) = cos²θ - sin²θ = 2cos²θ - 1 = 1 - 2sin²θ
tan(2θ) = 2tan θ / (1 - tan²θ)

Half Angle Formulas

These express trigonometric functions of θ/2 in terms of θ:

sin(θ/2) = ±√[(1 - cos θ) / 2]
cos(θ/2) = ±√[(1 + cos θ) / 2]
tan(θ/2) = sin θ / (1 + cos θ) = (1 - cos θ) / sin θ

Sum to Product Formulas

Convert sums of trigonometric functions into products:

sin α + sin β = 2 sin[(α + β)/2] cos[(α - β)/2]
sin α - sin β = 2 cos[(α + β)/2] sin[(α - β)/2]
cos α + cos β = 2 cos[(α + β)/2] cos[(α - β)/2]
cos α - cos β = -2 sin[(α + β)/2] sin[(α - β)/2]

Product to Sum Formulas

Convert products of trigonometric functions into sums:

sin α sin β = ½[cos(α - β) - cos(α + β)]
cos α cos β = ½[cos(α - β) + cos(α + β)]
sin α cos β = ½[sin(α + β) + sin(α - β)]

Applications

  • Engineering: Solving phase problems in electrical engineering
  • Physics: Analyzing wave interference and superposition
  • Calculus: Simplifying integrals and derivatives
  • Signal Processing: Breaking down complex waveforms

Example: Using Addition Formula

Find sin(75°) using the addition formula:

  • sin(75°) = sin(45° + 30°)
  • = sin(45°)cos(30°) + cos(45°)sin(30°)
  • = (√2/2)(√3/2) + (√2/2)(1/2)
  • = (√6 + √2) / 4

Tips for Using Identities

  • Always check which form of an identity is most useful for your problem
  • Remember the signs: addition formulas have different signs for sine and cosine
  • Use double angle formulas when you need to simplify expressions with 2θ
  • Half angle formulas are useful when working with angles like 15°, 22.5°, etc.
